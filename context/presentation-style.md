# Presentation Style Guide

## Purpose

Define a consistent, behavior-driven presentation style for:
- resident education
- faculty development
- CME / leadership presentations

Prioritize clarity, practicality, and real-world application.

---

## Core Rules

All presentations must:

- translate concepts into observable behavior
- connect behavior to outcomes (team, patient, workflow)
- emphasize ownership over task completion
- prioritize clarity over completeness
- be immediately usable in real environments

---

## Tone

Use a tone that is:

- clear and direct
- practical, not academic
- confident but not overly formal
- focused on behavior and action

Do not:

- use textbook or abstract language
- over-explain concepts
- use vague phrasing (“be professional”, “communicate better”)

---

## Slide Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Each slide must:

- contain one idea only
- use no more than 5 bullets
- avoid dense text
- avoid long paragraphs
- focus on clarity over detail

---

## Slide Structure

Each slide must include:

- Title (clear and specific)
- 3–5 bullets (short, action-focused)
- Speaker notes (expand behavior)

---

## Slide Count Guidance

- 30–45 minute session: 12–18 slides
- Short session (15–20 min): 6–10 slides

Do not produce fewer than 6 slides or more than 20 unless explicitly requested.

---

## Default Slide Pattern

When structure is not specified:

1. Concept
2. What it looks like in practice
3. Common mistakes
4. High-impact behaviors
5. Takeaway

---

## Content Patterns

### Behavior-Based Framing

Always describe behavior, not theory.

Bad:
- “Professionalism is important in clinical environments”

Good:
- “Professionalism is how you show up when things get busy”

---

### Contrast Framing

Use comparisons:

- Intern vs Senior
- Reactive vs Proactive
- Tasks vs Outcomes

---

### Ownership Framing

Reinforce:

- ownership of outcomes
- responsibility for team performance
- proactive problem-solving

---

### Communication as Professionalism

Highlight:

- early communication
- clear expectations
- closed-loop follow-up
- respectful tone under pressure

Use language like:

- “I wanted to flag this early…”
- “Let’s regroup at [time]…”
- “Can you take this and update me by [time]?”
- “Just confirming this was completed”

---

## Leadership Framing

Always reinforce:

- your behavior sets the tone
- others model your actions
- leadership is operational, not positional

---

## Teaching Approach

Always:

- use real scenarios
- show consequences of behavior
- tie actions to team and patient outcomes
- focus on what to do, not just what to know

---

## Use of Frameworks

Frameworks must:

- simplify behavior
- not increase complexity

Use frameworks to:

- explain behavior patterns
- diagnose issues
- guide leadership decisions

Do not introduce frameworks unless they improve clarity.

---

## Audience Calibration

Residents:
- direct
- practical
- behavior-focused

Faculty:
- structured but practical

CME / Leadership:
- concise
- outcome-focused
- decision-oriented
- respect existing expertise

---

## Speaker Notes

Speaker notes must:

- be 2–4 sentences per slide
- expand behavior from bullets
- include at least one concrete example or phrasing
- remain conversational
- not repeat slide bullets

Example:

Slide Bullet:
- Communicate early

Speaker Notes:
- This means not waiting until something becomes a problem. For example, “I wanted to flag this early so we can adjust before it escalates.” That kind of communication prevents downstream issues.

---

## Design Rules

Design must support clarity, not decoration.

### Layout

- simple vertical flow
- consistent spacing
- no clutter

---

### Text

- readable at a glance
- short lines
- scannable bullets

---

### Visual Hierarchy

Each slide must clearly show:

- title (most prominent)
- key points (secondary)
- speaker notes (supporting)

---

### HTML Structure (Required)

All HTML output must follow:

- each slide wrapped in <section>
- title uses <h2>
- bullets use <ul>/<li>
- speaker notes use <p>

Example:

<section>
  <h2>Slide Title</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Bullet 1</li>
    <li>Bullet 2</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Speaker notes here</p>
</section>

---

### Visual Defaults

- white background
- black text
- no color styling unless requested
- no external CSS or JavaScript
- minimal inline styling only for spacing

---

## Do Not Do

- do not use academic language
- do not define concepts without behavior
- do not exceed 5 bullets
- do not include multiple ideas per slide
- do not use vague language
- do not create content that cannot be applied immediately

---

## Final Output Filter

Before finalizing:

- simplify language
- remove unnecessary words
- convert abstract → behavior
- confirm one idea per slide
- confirm bullets are actionable
- confirm tone matches real-world clinical environment

---

## Agent Output Schema

All presentations must:

- stay within slide count guidance
- follow required HTML structure
- include speaker notes for every slide
- maintain consistent structure across slides
- reflect behavior-based teaching

Validate before finalizing:

- structure is consistent
- tone matches guide
- no academic phrasing
- content is usable immediately

---

## Goal

Presentations must be:

- clear
- practical
- behavior-driven
- immediately usable
- consistent
